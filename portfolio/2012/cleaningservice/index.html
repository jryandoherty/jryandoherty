Commercial cleaning services

Professional cleaning companies provide a multitude of services for your business. You can pick and choose select services you need or request a package service that includes all basic services and some additional detailing.

A commercial cleaning service can offer the following services for your business:

  • Trash pickup
  • Vacuuming and mopping common areas
  • Sweeping debris
  • Dusting
  • Basic interior window washing
  • Restroom maintenance
  • Replenishing consumables

When you work with a commercial cleaning service, you can pick the frequency of services depending on the size of your business. If you operate a small business with only a select number of employees, you may only need one cleaning professional who takes out the trash a few times a week. Larger businesses may need multiple cleaners to handle daily trash pickup, vacuuming and mopping, and other services to keep the office looking good as new.

Special services

Another reason you need to work with commercial cleaners is to receive special services. While employees can bring a trash can to the curb or change the soap dispenser in the bathroom, you can’t expect them to scrub the outside of your building or clean the parking lot.

Commercial cleaning services might also include an array of special services that take several hours (or days) depending on your project needs. Always provide advanced notice to arrange a time that will get the job done without distracting your employees. Among the special services available:

  • Washing exterior windows (using the right equipment and taking appropriate safety measures)
  • Dusting ceilings and light fixtures
  • Cleaning the roof and gutters
  • Pressure washing the building exterior
  • Maintaining HVAC equipment
  • Cleaning parking lots and garages
  • Dry foam carpet extraction
  • Intensely focused cleaning jobs (“Q-Tip cleaning”)

Licensed and insured

Here’s a final benefit to keep in mind about working with professional cleaning services: they are fully licensed and insured. If you have expensive marble or wood floors that you don’t want damaged, or expensive equipment you’re worried may “disappear,” you’ll be covered for loss and damage under their policies.
