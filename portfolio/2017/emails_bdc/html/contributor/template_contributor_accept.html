Editorial Guidelines

The Business.com mission is to provide instructional, highly actionable content that also entertains and engages our audience, better enabling people to run and grow their businesses and careers.Â  Fueling this mission is the belief that business people today are far more interested in learning how to do something, than in simply reading about something.

The following criteria is designed to help guide our contributors to submit content that is in synchrony with our mission. The following Business.com editorial guidelines apply to all content published on and distributed by Business.com

  • Actionable. Content on Business.com should not just provide the “why” but the “how.”
  • Instructional. Content on Business.com should provide the instructions for implementation including step-by-step guidelines, tips, screenshots and/or products and services that apply to the article topic.
  • Data-driven. Each post on Business.com should have at least two relevant data points or statistics that serve to provide market validation for your content’s positioning.
  • A minimum of 700 words, but are ideally 800-1000+ words.
  • Original. Content that has been published elsewhere will not be accepted.
  • Scannable. Use sub-headers, bullets and bolding to aid in making your article scannable.
  • Writing Standards. We only publish writing that is of high quality and ethics. You can use the instructions here to learn how to access the readability tools in Microsoft Word, which can give you an idea as to the quality of your content. In general, your post should have:
    • Passive sentences: Less than 10% (the lower the better).
    • Flesch Reading Ease: At least 40 (the higher, the better).
    • Flesch-Kincaid Grade Level: At least 10. This assessment does not measure the grade level of your writing, but rather the grade level of the reader.

Linking Rules

You are allowed one in-text link back to your site, and one link within your author bio. If we determine the link is unnecessary, it will be removed. All links included must be used for reference, and clearly identifiable to the reader what they’re being linked to.

Editing and Publishing

As editors, we reserve the right to edit your content for the Business.com audience, or send your pieces back for revisions to better fit our audience. In most cases, articles will reviewed within 48 hours and will be published within 2 weeks of delivery, but this is not guaranteed.

Social Sharing & Engagement

We strongly encourage you to share content on your social channels, and engage with others that are sharing and interacting with your content. Not only does this help your brand get more exposure, but it drives views to your article.
